Who finds their way here

You're not failing. By most measurements, you're succeeding — the career, the freedom, the version of yourself you once worked hard to become. And still, Sunday nights carry a particular weight. Something underneath all of it that won't stay quiet.

You've read the books. Done the therapy. Had the conversations. You understand yourself. And yet understanding hasn't moved you. You're still orbiting the same edges, still choosing the slightly smaller version of things — the safer answer, the more palatable truth, the life that fits inside what people expect.

You know something is waiting on the other side of the thing you keep not doing. The thing you keep almost saying. The version of yourself you keep postponing.

You're not here by accident.

The Work

This isn't coaching as you've experienced it.
This is going somewhere.

Most change lives in the head. It stays there as insight, as language, as the very sophisticated story you tell about yourself. Elegant. Articulate. Unchanged.

The Edge Method works differently. We go to the places that insight can't reach — the patterns that run beneath awareness, the identities that protect you by keeping you small, the parts of yourself you've locked away because they didn't fit the life you were building.

This work is direct. Sometimes uncomfortable. Always in service of you. You'll be seen clearly — not fixed, not managed, but genuinely met — and guided toward the edge of who you've been willing to be.

What happens at that edge is yours.

How we work

Three dimensions of the same truth.

I

See Clearly

Before anything can change, you need an honest map. Not a flattering one — an accurate one. We surface the patterns, the avoidances, the identities you've outgrown, and the stories you mistake for truth.

II

Move Differently

Insight without action is just a more sophisticated cage. The work always lands somewhere real — a conversation you have, a decision you make, a way you show up that you've never tried before.

III

Become Someone New

Identity-level change is slow, then sudden. We're not optimizing behavior — we're dissolving the beliefs that made those behaviors feel necessary. This is where real freedom lives.

Max — Oka Haus

I'm not here to give you answers.
I'm here to follow you into the questions you've been avoiding.

I came to this work through my own version of what you're describing — a life that looked right from the outside while something essential stayed hidden, even from me. The turning point wasn't insight. It was the first time I told a truth I'd been swallowing for years.

I've spent three years in the most intimate rooms of people's lives — sitting with the things they've never said out loud, the parts they've made convenient, the versions of themselves they keep almost becoming. This work changes me too.

I believe in gentleness and directness as the same thing. I believe the darkness you've been avoiding is exactly where your next life is waiting. And I believe most people are far more capable of courage than they've been allowed to discover.

I'm based in Bangkok. I work with people everywhere.
